2. Top-Rope climbing

Top-rope climbing involves a rope that is anchored at the top of the climbing route. The climber is belayed from below by a partner (or from above, in some cases). The rope runs through an anchor at the top of the climb, providing continuous protection against falls.

3. Lead climbing

Lead climbing is a type of climbing where the climber ascends a route while attaching themselves to the rope via a series of quickdraws (devices that clip the rope to the wall).
